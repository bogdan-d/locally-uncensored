Image-to-Image with Local AI

Published April 10, 2026 · PurpleDoubleD · 5 min read

Text-to-image is great for creating from scratch. But what if you already have a photo and want AI to transform it? That is image-to-image: you upload an existing image, write a prompt describing what you want, and the AI produces a new version that blends your original with the prompt.

With Locally Uncensored v2.3.0, image-to-image runs entirely on your machine. No uploads to cloud servers, no content filters, no usage limits. This guide covers how it works, which models to use, and how to get the best results.

How Image-to-Image Works

In text-to-image, the model starts from pure noise and shapes it into an image guided by your prompt. Image-to-image is different: it starts from your uploaded photo, adds a controlled amount of noise, then denoises it back while following your prompt. The result keeps the structure of your original image but transforms the content.

The key parameter is denoise strength (0.0 to 1.0):

  • Low denoise (0.1 - 0.3) keeps most of your original image. Good for subtle style changes, color correction, or adding small details.
  • Medium denoise (0.4 - 0.6) balances original structure with new content. Best for style transfer, changing clothing, or altering backgrounds.
  • High denoise (0.7 - 1.0) almost completely regenerates the image. Only the rough composition survives. Good for dramatic transformations.

How to Use I2I in Locally Uncensored

Open the app, go to the Create tab (Image tab), and switch to Image to Image in the right panel. That is it. No ComfyUI workflow editing, no node graphs, no JSON configs.

  • Upload: Drag and drop an image into the upload zone, or click to browse.
  • Prompt: Describe the transformation you want.
  • Denoise: Adjust the slider. Start at 0.5 and go from there.
  • Generate: Hit Generate or Ctrl+Enter.

The workflow selection stays on Auto. The app automatically builds the right ComfyUI pipeline: LoadImage, VAEEncode, KSampler with your denoise value, VAEDecode, SaveImage. You never touch a node.

Tips for Better Results

Match the prompt to your denoise level

At low denoise (0.2), a vague prompt works because the original image does most of the work. At high denoise (0.8), you need a detailed prompt because the model is generating most of the content fresh.

Use negative prompts

Click "Negative prompt" below the main prompt. Add things like "blurry, low quality, watermark, deformed" to steer the model away from common artifacts.

Try different models for different tasks

SDXL models (Juggernaut, DreamShaper) handle photorealistic transformations well at lower VRAM. FLUX models produce more creative interpretations. Z-Image Turbo has zero content restrictions.

Start with medium denoise

0.5 is a safe starting point. If the result looks too much like the original, increase it. If it looks nothing like the original, decrease it. The sweet spot is usually 0.4 to 0.7.

I2I vs Cloud Services

Cloud services like Midjourney or DALL-E offer image editing, but with restrictions. Content filters block certain prompts. Your images are uploaded to external servers. Usage is metered and costs money.

Running I2I locally means your images stay on your machine. There are no content filters. You can run it as many times as you want. The only cost is electricity and a GPU with at least 6 GB VRAM.
